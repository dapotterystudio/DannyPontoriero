Slow Sunday Ragu

Rome, Italy

Origin note

A family lunch dish remembered as much by timing and smell as by ingredient list.

Narrative

The best version of this recipe starts long before lunch. It belongs to the rhythm of a home rather than a dinner slot: onions softening, a red wine bottle already open, someone asking when the pasta water should go on.

In the archive, this context matters. It is what turns a recipe from data into memory and helps connect the dish to the people, place, and pace that shaped it.

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, 2 carrots, 2 celery stalks
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 pounds beef chuck
  • 1 pound Italian sausage
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cans whole peeled tomatoes
  • Bay leaves, Parmesan rind, salt, pepper

Method

  1. Cook the soffritto slowly until fully soft and fragrant.
  2. Brown the meat in stages for real depth.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, deglaze with red wine, then add tomatoes and aromatics.
  4. Simmer gently for about 3 hours, adjusting with water when needed.
  5. Finish pasta in the sauce so it tastes integrated, not topped.
