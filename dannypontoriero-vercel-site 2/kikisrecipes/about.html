About the concept

A food website designed to feel like a personal archive with the logic of a modern database.

KIKISRECIPES is built around one core belief: recipes matter more when they are tied to place, memory, and cultural context.

What makes the site different

Instead of separating recipes from travel writing, the archive brings them together. A dish can be contextualized by the city it came from, the restaurant where it was discovered, or a story recorded on the same trip.

This creates a scalable content model that works for editorial publishing now and can later expand into saved collections, user submissions, media galleries, or collaborative food maps.

Structured fields

  • Title and entry type
  • City, region, and country
  • Tag-based discovery
  • Narrative context
  • Structured recipe or place notes
